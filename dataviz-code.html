The above graph represents the trajectory of corruption indices for 10 countries over time. We can observe that Saudi Arabia seems to have experienced an improvement in the corruption score overtime, while Turkey observes the steepest decline among all countries. Turkey currently suffers from an annual inflation rate of 20.3% and the government in a bid to retain its power decided to boost consumer credit by subsidizing fruits and vegetables in the wholesale market in the run-up to the 2019 elections. Not surprisingly, it observes a relatively high Ease of Doing Business score, expressed in Plot 3, because the government has the power to strong-arm the local and state-owned banks to keep the lending rates artifically low so that the corporate sector backing the incumbent government stands to benefit from this arrangement (Bircan and Saka 2017).

Robust institutions and business environment go hand in hand. Lower level of corruption is associated with higher level of entrepreneurial opportunities in countries with higher ranking in ease of doing business.

Since CPI is a subjective assessment of the level of corruption perceived by businesspersons, and how it effects on business, exploring the above relation was crucial as it offers some new insights on how corruption and regulatory environment impact businesses.

Strong institutions can be characterized as having low bribery rates, lesser red tape, favorable investment opportunities, lower occurences of fraud, and high penalities for illegal practices, among other metrics.

In addition to measuring the costs and time it would take a given company to start a business, get credit, pay taxes, and enforce contracts, the score also measures the level of protection for investors. Investor confidence is bound to improve in countries with sound government institutions and enforcement mechanisms under which businesses thrive.

Thus, the above graph becomes important is reflecting how FDI flows have improved across all the specified countries. Saudi Arabia experiences the largest proportional increase in FDI outflows between 2012 and 2018. The United States, on the other hand, sees a negative growth in FDI outflows in 2018, relative to 2012. Afghanistan is still characterized as a fragile state owing to lack of a good governance structure. This instability in addition to worsening economic conditions, and increasing corruption incidence has undermined investments, in turn, experiencing one of the lowest FDI flows.

Thus, good governance and low corruption incidence in a country is essential to build an environment for to investors to pump money into lucrative projects and for businesses to thrive, in turn, contributing to higher economic growth.
